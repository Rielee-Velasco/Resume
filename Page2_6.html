Lead Confirmation Procedure

  1. Greet the customer.

    The first step in the lead confirmation procedure is to greet the customer warmly and professionally. This sets the tone for the rest of the interaction and establishes a positive relationship with the customer. A friendly greeting can help put the customer at ease and make them more receptive to the rest of the conversation. It's important to greet the customer by their name, and to introduce yourself and our company. This helps establish a personal connection and makes the interaction more personable.

    Here are some examples of scripts to start the call:

    • Hey there, this is Brad calling from ABC Company. How are you doing today?
    • This is Brad with ABC Company. Thanks for taking my call. How are you doing?

  2. Confirm the date, time, and address of the appointment.

    Confirming the date, time, and address of the appointment is an essential step in ensuring that we are on the same page as the customer. It shows that we value the customer's time and that we are organized and efficient in delivering our service. When confirming the appointment details, it's important to double-check the date, time, and address to avoid any confusion or miscommunication. This also gives the customer the opportunity to make any necessary changes or adjustments if there are conflicts in their schedule or if there are any unforeseen circumstances.

    Here are some examples of scripts to confirm the details of the appointment:

    • So we're just making sure that you and your spouse are available for us on [date] [time] at[location/address] . That's a good time for you guys, right?
    • I'm calling to double-check our appointment details. We're all set for [date] at [time] at [location/address]. That schedule works for you and your spouse, yes?

    3. It’s important to phrase this question in a way that includes the spouse’s availability in preparation for the next question.

  3. Confirm that all decision makers will be present at the time of the visit.

    By confirming the presence of all decision-makers, we avoid any last-minute changes or delays that may arise due to the absence of a decision-maker. This helps to ensure a smooth and successful lead appointment, reducing the likelihood of any potential complications or obstacles that may arise during the sales process.

    Here are some examples of scripts to confirm the availability of decision makers:

    • Just to confirm, will all decision makers be present for the appointment on [date] at [time]?
    • I just want to make sure that we'll be able to speak to everyone who needs to be involved. Are all decision makers available for the appointment on [date] at [time]?
    • Just so we can avoid any delays in the process, will [date] at [time] work for all the decision makers?

  4. Confirm that there will be enough time for the service to be done.

    It's crucial to confirm that there will be enough time for the service to be done and it's important to ensure that the customer's expectations are managed and that the service can be completed efficiently.

    • Just to double-check, we're not getting in the way of any prior engagements, right? Like any special plans or events such as skydiving, brunch, or dinner?

  5. Confirm the service that the customer requested and prioritize it.

    Verifying the specific service that the customer requires is crucial to ensure that the appropriate service is provided and given priority during the appointment. Depending on how you phrase it, this could also be an opportunity for us to make our customer aware of the availability of the other services we offer.

    Here is an example script to verify the service that the user is requesting for:

    • Just to make sure, we're coming for the roof, right? That's the priority? Or was it the roof and the windows?

    In this script, even though we already know that the customer is requesting for the service on their roof, we mention the windows to effectively prompt the customer about other available services, which can be an opportunity for upselling.

  6. Inquire about the utility bill and confirm that it is over $85 per month.

    Keep this simple. A one-liner like below should do the trick:

    • What are you guys paying every two months?

  7. Ask if there are any existing liens, bankruptcies, or forbearances on the property.

    The timing is vital for this question. Ask this immediately after getting the number for the utility bill question.

    • Any liens, bankruptcies, or forbearances there?
      • If there is a bankruptcy, inquire about when it happened and which chapter was filed. Was it Chapter 7 or Chapter 11?
      • If there is a lien, inquire about the details, including who placed the lien and the reason for it.
      • If the customer has a lien, inform them that they might not qualify for two of the three programs, and a sales representative will check for other available options.
      • If there is a forbearance, inquire about the details, including when it happened and if the customer has the letter.

Note:

The order of the questions is not all that important. The important thing is to ask them all because that will ensure that the job is done on the scheduled date with no delays due to the decision-makers not being available. This also ensures that we are able to determine the appropriate financial plan for the customer.
